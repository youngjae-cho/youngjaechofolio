Direct Preference Optimization (DPO) and related methods align LLMs from pairwise preferences by regularizing updates against a fixed reference policy. However, as the policy drifts, a static reference becomes increasingly miscalibrated, leading to distributional mismatch and amplifying spurious preference signals under noisy supervision. Reference-free variants avoid mismatch but often suffer from unconstrained reward drift.

We propose **Geometric Anchor Preference Optimization (GAPO)**, which replaces the fixed reference with a dynamic, geometry-aware anchor: an adversarial local perturbation of the current policy within a small radius that serves as a pessimistic baseline.

**Key Contributions:**

- **Dynamic Geometric Anchor**: Constructed via adversarial perturbation of model parameters within an l2 ball, serving as a pessimistic local surrogate that adapts as the policy evolves
- **Anchor Gap**: A novel metric measuring the reward discrepancy between the policy and its anchor, enabling instance-wise reweighting that downweights geometrically brittle instances
- **Theoretical Foundation**: Proved that the Anchor Gap approximates worst-case local margin degradation under smoothness conditions, connecting it to local sharpness and margin sensitivity
- **Geometric Data Valuation**: Demonstrated that the Anchor Gap can serve as an implicit data curation mechanism, identifying stable vs. brittle supervision signals

**Results:**

- Consistently outperformed DPO, SimPO, IPO, KTO, CPO, ORPO, R-DPO, and alpha-DPO on AlpacaEval 2.0 and Arena-Hard across Mistral-7B, Llama-3-8B, and Gemma-2-9B
- Improved robustness under both random and structured (length-dependent) label noise
- Reduced alignment tax: maintained reasoning performance (GSM8k, ARC-C) while improving alignment
- Reduced hallucination behavior and improved factual grounding compared to reference-free baselines
- Achieved flatter loss landscape (lower Hessian eigenvalues) indicating better generalization

This work bridges geometric stability (inspired by sharpness-aware optimization) and preference-based alignment, proposing that local geometric resilience is a practical proxy for the reliability of preference supervision.
