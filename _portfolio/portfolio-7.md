## Summary

Team **Pyler** won the **Domain-Specialized Model (Track B)** at the **NVIDIA Nemotron Hackathon**, held at *NVIDIA Nemotron Developer Day Seoul 2026*. Our entry was a precision analysis system for **video safety evaluation**, built on NVIDIA's latest lightweight Nemotron LLM.

> **Role:** Member of the Pyler ML team — ML Research Scientist.

## What We Built

A video-safety evaluation system that goes beyond *describing* a clip: it **classifies harmful content** faster and more accurately than general video-LLMs, and **explains its reasoning logically** — citing the specific evidence in the clip that grounded each decision.

- **Backbone** — NVIDIA's latest lightweight Nemotron LLM
- **Specialization** — fine-tuned for the policy taxonomy used in production video safety review
- **Outputs** — (i) harmful-content classification, (ii) rationale grounded in the visual evidence
- **Why it matters** — auditable decisions that reviewers can verify, not opaque blackbox labels

## Why the Judges Liked It

- Higher precision and recall on harmful-content categories than off-the-shelf video-LLM baselines
- Logically structured rationales that align with the policy taxonomy
- Latency profile compatible with at-scale production review
